01 — Overview

What is the Time Cycle
Bracket Tool?

A visual aid used to identify the energetic rhythm of money flowing in and out of a market. Every security and currency has its own unique rhythm — this is Hurst's Principle of Cyclicality.

The bracket is not the cycle itself. The cycles appear on the actual price chart. The bracket simply provides an idealised visual framework to help you see when buying or selling pressure is likely to shift.
Getting the correct bracket placement gives you a significant edge in the marketplace. But it is as much an art form as it is a science — you are developing a feel for how energy is shifting, guided by Hurst's principles.

02 — Foundations

The Four Hurst
Principles

The bracket tool is grounded in four principles developed by J.M. Hurst after years of research. Together they explain why markets move in rhythmic, quasi-predictable patterns.

PRINCIPLE 01
Cyclicality

All markets move in rhythmic cycles driven by money flow. No market movement is truly random — all price action can be broken down into component cycles of varying lengths. Every security has its own unique rhythmic signature.

Foundation
PRINCIPLE 02
Variation

Cycles will never be a perfect fit. You must always expect some degree of variation in when lows actually occur. Real-world cycles deviate ±10–15% from their nominal period. The bracket tells you when to be alert, not the exact date.

Critical caveat
PRINCIPLE 03
Harmony

Cycles come in twos or fours. A major cycle subdivides into 2 lower-degree cycles, which each subdivide into 2 minor harmonics — 4 total. Hurst's nominal model includes cycles of 10-day, 20-day, 40-day, 80-day, 20-week, and 40-week periods related by multiples of 2.

Nesting structure
PRINCIPLE 04
Synchronicity

When multiple cycles converge at the same point, you get either the deepest corrections (all pointing down) or the most powerful advances (all pointing up). This explains why market bottoms produce aggressive V-shaped lows while tops take longer to form.

Confluence signal
03 — Method

The Eight-Step
Process

Apply these steps in sequence every time you analyse a market from scratch. The order matters — always top-down, always trough to trough.

Start on the weekly chart

STEP 01 — HIGH TIMEFRAME FIRST

Higher-degree dominant cycles exert far more influence over shorter ones. You must establish the big picture before zooming in. The weekly chart reveals multi-year rhythms that the daily chart hides entirely. Never begin on a lower timeframe — you will misread the cycle degree.

Fact — all sources agreeTop-down mandatory
→Open the weekly chart of your instrument — Gold, FX pair, stock index
→Set zoom to show at least 5–10 years of price history
→Dominant cycles on weekly charts span 1.5–4 years in the Hurst nominal model
→Do not start on a daily or hourly chart — cycle degree identification will be wrong
The instructor in the source material is explicit: "I always start with the weekly chart when analysing from scratch." Higher timeframes anchor everything below them.

Identify key cycle lows by eye

STEP 02 — HUMAN PATTERN RECOGNITION

Scan the weekly chart for major pivot troughs — points where price made a significant low and reversed strongly upward. This is deliberately done by human eye, not by automated software. A computer uses only prior data and cannot adapt to what is unfolding in real time.

Fact — PDF explicit: by eye onlySubjectivity is intentional
→Look for the most obvious, significant troughs — not every minor wiggle
→Confirm the reversal was strong — price moved sharply higher after the low
→Mark 2–4 candidate lows as starting anchors
→Gold example: major lows at end-2008, end-2015/early-2016, and late 2022
→Ambiguous lows get resolved later via shorter-cycle analysis fed back upward
Hurst phasing analysis works top-down first, then bottom-up. Mark the longest cycle lows first, then use shorter cycles to resolve any ambiguity in placement.

Place the bracket trough to trough

STEP 03 — THE CORE MEASUREMENT

The bracket always anchors from one confirmed cycle low to the next. Cycles are measured trough to trough — never peak to peak. The bracket width equals the estimated cycle period. This is the single most fundamental rule in Hurst's method.

Fact — all Hurst sources confirm
Solid vertical lineMajor cycle low — high timeframe anchor point
Bold dashed lineMid-cycle correction zone — halfway pullback expected
Grey dashed lineMinor degree harmonics — quarter-cycle reference points

Add mid-cycle dashed lines

STEP 04 — HALFWAY MARKERS

Mark the halfway point of each bracket with a bold dashed vertical line. This is where a temporary mid-cycle correction is expected before the next leg higher. Real lows will come slightly early or late — the Principle of Variation makes this normal and expected.

Fact — PDF confirmedAllow ±10–15% timing variation
→Calculate the midpoint in time between your two anchor lows
→Draw a bold dashed vertical line at that midpoint
→This marks a corrective zone — not a major cycle low, just a pullback
→Add lighter grey dashed lines at the quarter points (minor harmonics)
→In the Gold analysis, mid-cycle dashed lines tagged actual lows multiple times with minor variation
Treat the dashed line as a zone, not an exact date. Price may turn a few weeks early or late. That is not a failure of the tool — it is the Principle of Variation working as expected.

Apply the Principle of Harmony

STEP 05 — BUILD THE HARMONIC FAMILY

Every dominant cycle contains a harmonic family. One major cycle subdivides into 2 lower-degree cycles, each of which subdivides into 2 minor cycles — giving 4 minor cycles nested inside each dominant bracket.

Fact — PDF + Hurst nominal model
→Draw a second, smaller bracket layer on top of your major bracket
→Gold example: dominant 2022–2029 → 2 major subdivisions → 4 minor harmonics
→Hurst nominal model reference: 10-day, 20-day, 40-day, 80-day, 20-week, 40-week
→Harmonics relate by multiples of 2 — never arbitrary ratios

Mark cycle convergence points

STEP 06 — SYNCHRONICITY IN ACTION

When multiple cycles align at the same point in time, this is cycle convergence — the most powerful signal in Hurst analysis. The number of cycles converging determines the magnitude of the resulting move.

Fact — PDF + all Hurst sources
Cycles converging DOWN Deep, sharp correction. More cycles converging = deeper the trough. Mark with × on chart. Gold late-2022: 3 cycles converged down — produced the major cycle low.
Cycles converging UP Powerful advance. After the 2022 low, all 3 cycles pointed up simultaneously — producing the explosive bull run through 2024–2025.
→Count how many cycles are due to trough at roughly the same date
→2 cycles converging = moderate move, 3+ cycles = significant move
→Gold current: 2 cycles converge around May 2026 → shallower mid-cycle low expected
→Mark convergence zones with × symbols on your chart for visual reference

Refine timing on the daily chart

STEP 07 — LOW TIMEFRAME REFINEMENT

After establishing the weekly cycle context, zoom into the daily chart to narrow the projected low date. The two timeframes should sync — the overlap zone becomes your highest-probability target window.

Fact — PDF specificVariation still applies at daily level
→Weekly chart → month-level window (e.g. "May 2026")
→Daily chart → week-level precision (e.g. "early June 2026")
→Gold example: weekly → May, daily → early June. Variation window: April to July
→When both timeframes sync → highest probability of an important pivot
Confirmation tools to use alongside: Fibonacci retracements, yearly S/R levels, pitchforks, S/R shifts, and correlated markets (e.g. US Dollar). The bracket tells you when to look; these tools tell you when the low is actually in.

Identify left vs right translation

STEP 08 — DIRECTIONAL BIAS

Once a cycle is underway, observe where the price peak falls within the bracket window. This reveals the cycle's directional bias and tells you how to treat any near-term rallies.

Fact — PDF + Hurst sources confirm
Left translated Peak falls in the FIRST half of the bracket. Bearish bias. Near-term rallies likely to fail before the final cycle low forms. Do not chase strength.
Right translated Peak falls in the SECOND half of the bracket. Bullish bias. Buy pullbacks — expect continuation higher after any correction.
→Gold current: left-translated — topped early with a ~21% drop
→Near-term rallies likely to fail before the May/June 2026 final low
→After the May/June low, Gold expected to enter a stronger bullish phase toward late 2027
The bracket is a visual rhythm framework, not a prediction machine. It tells you when to be alert for a shift in energy. The real edge comes from combining it with Fibonacci, S/R levels, pitchforks, and correlated markets to confirm the cycle has actually concluded.

06 — Complementary Tools

Never Use the Bracket
in Isolation

The bracket tool tells you when to be alert. These four complementary tools tell you when a cycle has actually concluded and a new phase of money flow has begun.

φ
Fibonacci Projections

Project price targets from confirmed cycle lows. Key levels include the 1-to-1 extension and the 0.66 retracement. Used to identify where the next leg higher is likely to stall or where a correction will find support.

—
Yearly S/R Levels

Key annual price levels (e.g. the 4,300 Gold level) used to find high-probability entry and exit zones. These levels act as natural magnets for cycle lows and highs and provide structural confirmation when they align with bracket timing.

↗
Pitchforks & S/R Shifts

Used to confirm when a cycle low has actually been set and energy has shifted from sellers to buyers. A break of a descending pitchfork or an S/R shift from resistance to support confirms the cycle turn without relying on time alone.

↔
Correlated Charts

Markets do not move in isolation. Analysing correlated instruments (e.g. US Dollar for Gold, Bonds for Equities) adds directional confluence. A weak Dollar structurally supports the bullish case for Gold — cycle timing aligns with macro context.
